I'm a Health Informatics student at Franklin University, Columbus, OH, completing my Master's degree (2024–2026) alongside my background as a Doctor of Pharmacy from Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Hyderabad.

My career spanned FDA-regulated Phase I–IV clinical trials across oncology, gastroenterology, and ophthalmology — managing patient recruitment, informed consent, eCRF data entry in Medidata Rave, Oracle Argus, and ArisGlobal, and ensuring full ICH-GCP compliance.

In my final year capstone, I'm collaborating with a team of 3 students to analyze real dental patient data for HW Dental (four Columbus-area branches), using Python, SQL, Excel, Tableau, and Power BI to surface marketing insights for Medicaid and Spanish-speaking populations.

I hold GCP certification, IATA dangerous goods training, BLS certification, and Medidata Rave EDC training, and have authored two peer-reviewed clinical pharmacology publications.

Capstone · Final Year · Team of 3

HW Dental — Healthcare Marketing Analytics

Marketing Analytics for a Growing Dental Chain

Analyzing de-identified patient data across four HW Dental branches in Central Ohio to surface demographic patterns, referral channel performance, and actionable marketing opportunities — especially for Medicaid and Spanish-speaking populations.

🏥 Live Client 👥 Team of 3 📍 Columbus, OH 🎓 BUSA 695

Project Overview

As part of Franklin University's Real-World Project initiative, our 3-person team partnered with HW Dental to perform end-to-end marketing analytics on a live, de-identified dataset from a fast-growing dental practice. I contribute across data cleaning, segmentation, and visualization using Python, SQL, Excel, Tableau, and Power BI.

Key Research Questions

  • Branch Comparison: How do Clintonville, Polaris, Sawmill & Westerville differ in patient demographics and insurance mix?
  • Referral Channels: Which sources (Google, personal, insurance) drive the most patients per branch?
  • Target Populations: How effectively is the clinic reaching Medicaid recipients and Spanish-speaking patients?
  • Geographic Gaps: Which Columbus-area ZIP codes remain underserved?
  • Communication Preferences: How can outreach be tailored by patient segment?
